D3 Quick Start

Viewport

In the previous slides you've seen that an SVG has a height and width attribute. In picture 1 the width and height define the grey rectangle, which is the physical space (in pixels) the SVG will occupy on the screen. This actual rendered size on the screen is called the Viewport.

parent container

The Viewort is not confined to its parent container and in picture 2 you can see the SVG spill over the edges of its parent container colored in orange. But anything drawn inside the SVG can only be seen within the confines of the Viewport. Shapes, like the green circles, that are placed on the fringes of the Viewport or too big for it are cropped. And when your missing an element, there is a good chance that it landed outside of the visible area.

You may address cropped or missing shapes by shifting them in the center of the Viewport (3-4), shrinking the shape (5-6) or enlarging the Viewport (7). There is also the viewBox attribute, that allows to zoom and pan the visible area inside the SVG. This is often not needed for D3 graphics and its size defaults to the size of the Viewport.
